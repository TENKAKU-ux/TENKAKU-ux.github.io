Real-time Video Translation Translate videos in the browser on the spot (Chrome extension)

It works broadly with any video that plays in Chrome, starting with YouTube and X. For videos that have captions, it reads the captions shown on screen and translates them with Chrome's built-in on-device translation; for videos without captions, it transcribes the tab's audio locally with Whisper and then translates. It can translate English, Chinese, and other languages into Japanese, and audio and text never leave the device — no external API. The original and the translation scroll side by side in the side panel, and you can save each translation record and export it as a Markdown (.md) file.

Where I got stuck, and how I got out

01

How to capture the tab's audio from the side panel. Even with permissions granted, the standard way to grab audio would not work.

I realized the permission is only granted at the instant a button inside the side panel is pressed, so I start audio-included screen sharing from that click.

02

Fetching YouTube's caption data directly had started returning empty bodies.

I switched to reading the captions shown on screen, and made it reliable on the assumption the viewer turns captions on.

03

The extension's background part gets killed after tens of seconds and forgets the target tab it was supposed to remember.

I stash the state in session storage and always reload it before continuing.
